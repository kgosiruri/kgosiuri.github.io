01 Profile

A quantitative researcher bridging the gap between rigorous Bayesian methodology and applied actuarial science. My work is motivated by a central question: how do we make principled decisions under genuine uncertainty, when data is sparse and expert knowledge must be formalised?

From Internal Model validation at Lloyd's syndicates to pricing analytics at Ageas UK, I have observed first-hand where classical methods break down — and where Bayesian frameworks open new possibilities.

02 Research Interests
01 — Expert Judgement
ε

Expert Judgement Elicitation & Aggregation

Formalising subjective expert knowledge when empirical data is sparse. Explores probabilistic Delphi methods, structured expert dependence modelling and translation into Internal Capital Models for emerging systemic and operational risks. Motivated by direct exposure to expert-judgement reliance during Internal Model validation at Faraday Underwriting.

02 — Dependency Modelling
C

Uncertainty Quantification & Vine Copulas

Capturing asymmetric, complex dependency structures in multivariate risk environments. Connects vine copula prior specification with minimum information methods and Extreme Value Theory to improve joint extreme event modelling in catastrophe and systemic risk frameworks.

03 — Decision Theory
π

Bayesian Risk Modelling & Decision Support

Developing robust probabilistic models for high-stakes decision-making under uncertainty. Investigates Bayes linear graphical models for optimal test strategy design, multi-attribute utility for reliability task allocation, and the translation of these frameworks into regulatory, pricing and risk-transfer applications.

04 — Machine Learning
β

Bayesian Additive Regression Trees

Bridging machine learning interpretability with rigorous Bayesian inference. Explores loss-based priors for BART tree topologies and integration with conditional copulas to model dynamic, state-dependent tail risks — with direct application to insurance pricing, reserving and systemic risk transfer.
